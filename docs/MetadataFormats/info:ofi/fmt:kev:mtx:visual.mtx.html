DRAFT Matrix defining the KEV Format to represent a Visual Material

dc:description This Matrix represents a single Visual Material item. This metadata format describes visual materials in a way consistent with how they are described by the library community.

A representation of a Key/Encoded-Value pair is generated by concatenating the contents of the first four columns of a row that begins with an ampersand in the Matrix below. The ordering of KEV pairs is not important. Rows which have '#' in the first column are comments and should not be included in the representation.

The following data types are provided for the values of the Keys, which must be URL-encoded:

<data> Character string
<id> Character string for an Identifier (Z39.88-2004, Part 1, Section 7)
<fmt-id> Character string for a Format Identifier (Z39.88-2004, Part 1, Sections 12 and 13)
<m-key> Character string for a Metadata Key (Z39.88-2004, Part 2, Section 7.1)
<url> Character string for a URL
<date> Character string representing a date to the complete date level of the W3CDTF profile of ISO 8601, of the form: [ YYYY-MM-DD | YYYY-MM | YYYY ]
<time> Character string representing a date and time to the seconds level of the W3CDTF profile of ISO 8601, of the form: [ YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ssTZD | YYYY-MM-DD ]

Abbreviations in column headings:

  • Delim - Delimiter
  • Min - minimum occurrence
  • Max - maximum occurrence ('*' = unbounded)

The Matrix

Delim Key Equals Value Min Max Description
& aulast = <data> 0 1 First author/creator's family name. This may be more than one word. In many citations, the author's family name is recorded first and is followed by a comma, e.g. Smith, Fred James is recorded as "aulast=smith". For a motion picture this could be a producer, director, actor, or author of screenplay.
& aufirst = <data> 0 1 First author/creator's given name or names or initials. This data element may contain multiple words and punctuation, e.g. "Fred F", "Fred James". For a motion picture this could be a producer, director, actor, or author of screenplay.
& auinit = <data> 0 1 First author/creator first and middle initials. For a motion picture this could be a producer, director, actor, or author of screenplay
& auinit1 = <data> 0 1 First author/creator's first initial. For a motion picture this could be a producer, director, actor, or author of screenplay
& auinitm = <data> 0 1 First author/creator's middle initial. For a motion picture this could be a producer, director, actor, or author of screenplay
& ausuffix = <data> 0 1 First author/creator's name suffix. Qualifiers on an author's name such as "Jr.", "III" are entered here. e.g. Smith, Fred Jr. is recorded as "ausuffux=jr". For a motion picture this could be a producer, director, actor, or author of screenplay
& au = <data> 0 * This data element contains the full name of a single author/creator, e.g. "Smith, Fred M", "Gene Roddenberry". For a motion picture this could be a producer, director, actor, or author of screenplay
& aucorp = <data> 0 1 Organization or corporation that is the author or creator of the book, e.g. "Scripps Institution of Oceanography."
& title = <data> 0 1 Title of the visual material.
& place = <data> 0 1 Place of publication. "New York"
& pub = <data> 0 1 Publisher name. "The Foundation"
& date = <date> 0 1 Date of publication. Publication dates are assumed to be a single year.
& edition = <data> 0 1 Statement of the edition of the visual material. This will usually be a phrase, with or without numbers, but may be a single number. E.g. "First edition", "4th ed."
& series = <data> 0 1 The title of a series in which the visual material was issued. There may also be an ISSN associated with the series.
& issn = <data> 0 1 International Standard Serials Number (ISSN). The issn may contain a hyphen, e.g. "1041-5653". An ISSN in the book format is often associated with a series title.
& isbn = <data> 0 1 International Standard Book Number (ISBN). The ISBN is usually presented as 9 digits plus a final check digit (which may be "X"), e.g. "057117678X" but it may contain hyphens, e.g. "1-878067-73-7"

"unknown"		 0 1 art: Reproductions of artworks. Generally prints but could be other medium as well.
chart: Generally wall charts used to depict large organizations, concepts, or systems.
filmstrip:A strip of film bearing a sequence of images for projection as still pictures.
motion: Refers to Motion Picture. A series of pictures projected on a screen in rapid succession. Stored on tape or digitally.
picture: A non-transparent 2-D visual image. Could be a photograph, poster, digital image, etc. Could be single or multiple images.
tech: Refers to Technical Drawings. Includes floor plans, architectural drawings, schematics, etc.
transparency: A picture on a transparent surface, such as photographic film, so that the picture can be projected onto a flat white surface. Often used for publishing photographs.
slide: An image on a transparent film, usually photographic, mounted on a rigid opaque frame. Often used to project to an audience.
video:Images recorded on videotape or on optical disc to be viewed on television screens, or the medium through which these images are recorded and displayed.
unknown: use when the genre of the visual material is unknown.

fcart: Film Cartridge. Film loops enclosed in cartridges.
fcassette: Film Cassette. Permanently encased film or tape that runs reel-to-reel.
freel: Film Reel. For motion pictures, indicates reel-to-reel, e.g., 8MM, 16MM, 35MM, etc.
GIF: Graphic Image File format.
JPEG: Joint Photographic Experts Group. Used to to refer to the standard they developed for still-image compression.
MPEG-1: Motion Picture Experts Group. Used to refer to an image compression scheme for motion-picture video they developed. MPEG-1 is least common denominator.
negative: Negative film roll or sheet.
positive: Positive film roll or sheet. Used for slides and transparencies.

VHS: Videocassette using the VHS format.
other: use when the media of the Visual Material is other.
